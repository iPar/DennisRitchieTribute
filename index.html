Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie

Father of C, UNIX and the Information Age

Receiving the Japan Prize in the category of Information and Communication (c. 2011)

Timeline of Dennis MacAlistair Ritchie's Life:

  • 1941 :  Born on the 9th of September in Bronxville, New York to Alistair E. Ritchie, a Bell Labs Scientist, and his wife

  • 1959 :  Graduates from Summit High School in Summit, New Jersey and begins undergraduate studies of Physics and Applied Mathematics at Harvard University in Cambridge, Massachusetts

  • 1967 :  Begins working at the Bell Laboratories Computing Sciences Research Center in Murray Hill, New Jersey

  • 1968 :  Defends his PhD thesis on "Program Structure and Computational Complexity" at Harvard University under the supervision of Patrick C. Fischer, though he never officially received his PhD

  • 1972 :  Releases first version of the C programming language with Bell Laboratories

  • 1973 :  Releases UNIX operating system publicly along with Ken Thompson, Brian Kernighan, Douglas McIlroy, and Joe Ossanna at Bell Laboratories

  • 1983 :  Receives the Turing Award along with Ken Thompson for their development of generic operating systems theory and specifically for their implementation of the UNIX operating system

  • 1990 :  Receives the IEEE Richard W. Hamming Medal from the Institute of Electrical and Electronics Engineers (IEEE), along with Ken Thompson, for the "origination of the UNIX operating system and the C programming language"

  • 1994 :  Receives the Computer Pioneer Award

  • 1997 :  Becomes a Computer History Museum Fellow, along with Ken Thompson, for "for co-creation of the UNIX operating system, and for development of the C programming language"

  • 1998 :  Jointly receives the National Medal of Techonology from President William Jefferson Clinton for co-inventing the UNIX operating system and the C programming language which, according to the medal "led to enormous advances in computer hardware, software, and networking systems and stimulated growth of an entire industry, thereby enhancing American leadership in the Information Age"

  • 2005 :  Awarded the Industrial Institute's Achievement Award in recognition of his contribution to science and technology, and to society generally, with his development of the UNIX operating system

  • 2011 :  Awarded the Japan Prize for Information and Communications, along with Ken Thompson, for their work in the development of the UNIX operating system

  • 2011 :  Dies on the 12th of October in Berkeley Heights, New Jersey at the age of 70.

Ritchie was under the radar. His name was not a household name at all, but... if you had a microscope and could look in a computer, you'd see his work everywhere inside.

~ Paul E. Ceruzzi, computer historian
